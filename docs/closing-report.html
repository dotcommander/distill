Who can judge? (calibration: discrimination + position bias)

Each judge is pitted against deterministic degradations of known-good digests (shuffle/truncate/cliché/listify) in both slot orders. Swap-robust accuracy = intact wins BOTH orders (guess ≈ 25%); position-bias = picks the same slot regardless of content (want ≤20%). A judge must be calibrated before its rankings are trusted.

Is glm-5.2's #1 real, or judge bias?

glm-5.2 recognizes its own output 8/8 (like opus & gpt-5.5) — a red flag for using it as a judge. But its #1 ranking is not self-inflated: the panels used leave-one-out (it never judged its own matches), and re-judging the fixed digests with a zero-stake panel (opus + gpt-5.5, neither in the field) on the merit criterion is TRUSTWORTHY (flip 9.4%) and still ranks glm-5.2 #1 (5-0-2). It recognizes its own writing because it genuinely is the best read, not from vanity.

Reframing the judge question from "which is best?" to "would you publish this?" reorders the field (kimi #1, gemma #2, glm-5.2 #4) — but that run is SUSPECT (flip rate jumps to 29.4% > 25%): publishability is a fuzzier, less order-robust criterion. Merit stays the trusted lens; the publish ranking is indicative only. (Side note: kimi tops both framings under neutral judges — a stronger digest writer than the original panel suggested.)

Headline. After de-biasing and the $8 output-cost filter, the two most expensive models (gpt-5.5, opus-4.8) win nothing they're worth paying for. glm-5.2's digest #1 is genuine (holds under zero-stake judges, not self-bias); deepseek-v4-pro is the best all-rounder AND the only trustworthy in-budget judge; kimi-k2.6 is the chosen writer. deepseek-v4-pro is the best value all-rounder; specialists beat generalists per task — gemini-flash-lite for label speed, kimi-k2.6 for comedy, glm-5.2 for reading.
